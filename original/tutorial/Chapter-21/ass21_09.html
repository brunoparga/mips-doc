Several Translations

The load immediate pseudoinstruction is useful for loading a register with an integer value. Here is another example: 

li  $t2,-156

This puts the two's complement representation of -15610 into register $t2. However, this can not be done with ori because the immediate field of that instruction must be a 16-bit unsigned integer (which is zero-extended to 32 bits upon execution).

Because the immediate value of the li is negative, a different translation is used: 

 
li $v2,-156   ==   addiu $v2,$0,-156

(Remember that addiu sign-extends its immediate operand before performing the addition. Also remember that the u part of the mnemonic means that overflow will not cause a trap. The addiu instruction can be used with negative integers).

The extended assembler translates li into different basic instructions depending on the sign of the immediate value.
